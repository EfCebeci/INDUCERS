## The 50-Year-Old Problem
For 50 years, academic analysis of the Indus script has been stuck on traditional frequency counts (N-Grams) of the symbols themselves. This is a "flat data" approach that has failed to yield conclusive results.

## Our Solution: The 'Hard Tech' Hack
We believe this is not just a linguistic problem; it is a **computational, large-scale data engineering problem.**

Our hypothesis is that the key to decipherment is hidden not in the *frequency* of symbols, but in their **structural and syntactic relationships**.

The academic community has already tagged linguistic data (e.g., the `.conll` format) with this rich structural information (like `deprel` and `upos` fields), but they lack the computational tools to leverage its full potential.

Our models (GNNs and Transformers) are the first to analyze this deep grammatical structure, moving from "what symbol is next?" to "why is this symbol the *subject* of that symbol?".

## Current Status & Proof (This Repo)
This repository is the live proof of our methodology.

1.  **Data Pipeline (`src/analysis/stats.py`):** This is our core Python script. It parses the complex, tagged `.conll` corpus (which we reverse-engineered) and uses libraries like `pandas` and `scikit-learn` to perform structural analysis.
2.  **Experimental Proof (`reports/`):** This folder contains the `.csv` files generated by our pipeline. These reports (e.g., `01_upos_frequency.csv`, `03_deprel_frequency.csv`) are our experimental proof that these rich, structural patterns exist and are quantifiable.
